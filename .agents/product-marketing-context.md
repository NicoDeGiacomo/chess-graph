## One-Liner

Free chess opening tree visualizer — explore variations as interactive graphs, import PGN, annotate moves, and master your repertoire in your browser.

## Tagline

Visualize Your Opening Repertoire

## Problem

Chess players struggle to organize and remember their opening preparation. PGN files are hard to read. Traditional tree views get overwhelming. Transpositions are invisible. And most tools require accounts and cloud sync.

## Solution

Chess Graph lets you build your repertoire visually as an interactive node-based graph. See your entire opening structure at a glance. Click to jump between positions. Add moves and watch the graph grow. Everything stays local in your browser.

## Key Differentiators

- **Visual graph view** — not just a list or tree; a real interactive node graph with auto-layout, zoom, pan, minimap
- **Board-graph sync** — click a node to update the board, make a move to grow the graph
- **Local-first privacy** — IndexedDB storage, data never leaves your device
- **No account required** — open the site, start building immediately
- **Transposition detection** — curved edges show when different move orders reach the same position
- **Free and open-source** — MIT license, no hidden costs ever

## Core Features

1. Interactive game tree (drag-drop, zoom, pan, minimap, collapse/expand subtrees)
2. Synced chess board (click nodes to jump, make moves to grow)
3. PGN import with full RAV support (multi-game merge, transposition detection)
4. Node customization (comments, 6 color codes, tags)
5. Board annotations (arrows, square highlights in orange/green/red)
6. Keyboard shortcuts (arrow navigation, algebraic move entry, undo/redo)
7. Repertoire management (multiple repertoires, folders, drag-and-drop)
8. JSON export/import for backup
9. Quick links to Chess.com and Lichess analysis
10. Dark/light theme

## Target Audience

| Segment | Use Case | Pain Point |
|---------|----------|------------|
